Which license?

Choosing a license

Consideration of which license to use for your project is a complex topic. The biggest factor to consider is with whom do you want to share your project? Even “free” (libre) and open source software have avenues from which to make a profit.

If you are planning to work within an existing open source project, then the best license to choose is the one used by that community.

Community Examples and Licenses:

Perl: Artistic License 2.0

Linux: GNU GPL 2.0 or 3.0

Eclipse: Eclipse Public License

OpenSolaris: Sun Common Development and Distribution License (CDDL)

Apache: Apache License

Firefox: Mozilla Public License

Advantages to this approach.

1) Mature License: when a license has been around for a while, multiple people have had a chance to review, revise and test the license (sometimes in court!)

2) Saves confusion: if you use a license different from everyone else, they may not know the inherent differences between licenses


If the license for the community is obscure (rare for a large community), then the BSD License or the GNU GPL are the best options, having been vetted by a large number of reviewers.

Other Considerations

If you are not inclined to follow the community (or don’t have one), then these considerations may guide you to the proper license:


Patent grant: Apache License, GNU GPL 2.0 and 3.0, Mozilla Public License, Eclipse License

Preserving your name and reputation: BSD License

Copyleft, giving as you receive: GNU GPL 2.0, ISC License, BSD License, Z Lib License, Creative Commons with Share Alike

Closed-source, “All Rights Reserved” development: Copyright, i.e. NO license

Customizable license by category: Creative Commons (Attribute, NonCommercial, No Derivatives, and/or Share Alike)


Overall, there is no wrong answer, but like your favorite flavor of ice cream, only you can decide which one to choose.
