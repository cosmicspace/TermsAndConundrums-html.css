Fair Use

What is Fair Use?

Fair use is a broad range of exceptions to Copyright that allows others to interact with or use a creator's work within a limited spectrum. Fair use is intended to be a series of limitations to copyright to balance the interests of copyright holders with the public interest by allowing fair use as a defense to copyright infringement claims for certain limited uses that might otherwise be considered infringement.

Fair Use examples

  • Commentary and discussion about the work
  • Criticism
  • Parody
  • Non-profit Educational
  • News reporting
  • Research
  • Scholarship
  • Search Engines, such as the use of thumbnails in image search engines

History and shenanigans

There are numerous cases where a content user will claim fair use in court. Due to the many angles and ways of looking at Fair Use, it isn't always clear what is and what is not Fair Use. Therefore, it is advised that any prospective content-user contact an attorney for insight into whether Fair Use would be a legitimate claim to use another's content.
