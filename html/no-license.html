No License

Definition and details

If no license is chosen, then the work is automatically copyrighted, and follows the normal rules of copyright. Even if the work is something as informal as a doodle on a napkin, it still counts as though it were under copyright.

When this should be used

This is by far the simplest of licensing options, since nothing needs to be done at all. However, this isn't generally considered to be a wise choice. For one, no intent can be surmised as to whether the work can be used by others, aside from Fair Use. A danger is that a copyright holder could later sue someone for using their work without permission. However, with no paperwork to denote the copyright holder, this can be a futile lawsuit. Nevertheless, developers would tend to steer clear of this ambiguity in order to protect themselves legally.

Furthermore, no mention of warranty is available when no license is chosen, so the creator or author could find themselves legally liable since they put forth no warnings about their work.
