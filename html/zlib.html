ZLib/LibPNG License

Definition and details

The ZLib/LibPNG license is not a very commonly used license, but is recognized by FOSS (Free Open Source Software) as a proper open source license. Containing the basic boilerplate license text, the owner of software licensed with the zlib/libpng license is absolved of any responsibility for damages caused by said software.

One of the things that makes the zlib/libpng license stand out is that it spends a lot of time discussing accreditation. It forbids the origins of the software from being misrepresented, and it encourages acknowledgment by people who use the software under this license. However, acknowledgment is not required.

The Zlib/LibPNG license finds itself in a nice middle ground between very permissive licenses such as the MIT license and much more restrictive licenses such as the GPL. Many critiques of permissive licenses such as the MIT is that corporations can simply copy the exact code and resell it for profit. The clause stating that the origin cannot be misrepresented makes doing this a lot more difficult.

This license bears a lot of similarities to the BSD license. Some differences is that the BSD license is more restrictive with its accreditation requirements, and the BSD license is actually a family of licenses, so simply mentioning "BSD license" can be unclear about exactly what that implies.

When this should be used

The Zlib license is ideal when you want a permissive license approved by teh Free Software Foundation (FSF), but want it to be very clear about the source of the software, with a focus on making sure it is known that your original software was altered. This license requres that this be clearly noted.

Length or term of license

Length of license corresponds to the work’s copyright. In the USA, this is typically 70 years after the death of the creator, or 120 after it was made by an organization, at which point, the work becomes public domain.
